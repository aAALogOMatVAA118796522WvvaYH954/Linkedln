    NewFrac Training Network

    Funded by the European Commission under a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions Grant Agreement n° 861061

      About the project
      A high-level training of a new generation of creative, entrepreneurial and innovative early-stage researchers (ESRs) through the development and engineering applications of a new modeling framework focused on the prediction and analysis of multi-field fracture phenomena in heterogeneous engineering systems at different scales. NEWFRAC in its mission of training students capable of solving the current problems of multi-field fracture phenomena in heterogeneous engineering systems, offers 13 PhD positions for early stage researchers (ESRs) distributed in a network of 5 European countries (France, Germany, Italy, Portugal and Spain) and 2 countries associated (Israel and Switzerland), with the participation of prestigious academic and industrial institutions that will allow researchers to grow and develop their technical skills in a multisectoral environment.

      Work Team / Consortium
    • 7 internationally recognized research groups as experts in the field of solid and fracture mechanics and expert doctoral trainers.
    • 5 leading high-tech companies interested in the developments and results of this network.

      Tasks involved
    • New computational methodologies based on the original formulations of CCFFM (w/discontinuous representation of cracks) and PF (w/regularized diffused representation of cracks) and the Principle of Minimum Total Energy subject to a Stress Condition will be developed and implemented in a FEM code to provide accurate and reliable predictions for complex mixed mode fracture problems involving simultaneous crack onset, propagation, and interactions with interfaces in anisotropic brittle materials.
    • New optimization procedures will be required, e.g., for CCFFM, a modified a staggered scheme aimed at global optimization in the feasible parameter-region given by the stress condition due to the efficiency of its computational implementation.
    • Novel forms of the degradation and local fracture energy functions will be proposed to impose stress conditions in PF. Staggered hybrid CCFFM and PF schemes, providing necessary information to the PF algorithm in terms of suitable boundary/interface conditions or prescribed values for PF damage variable in each time step, will be explored. Validation of the computational tools developed by their application to industrial problems.

    Master thesis: Safety of EV battery system

    Integration and simulative testing of an integrated crash structure within the side skirt to the power line within the vehicle body and to protect the battery system in the event of a lateral vehicle impact.

      About the project
      Electric vehicles are increasingly gaining attention as an alternative to fossil fuel vehicles, due to its emission free propulsion system, increased interest in fuel economy and environment in recent years. As a clean energy technology, it requires to meet unique safety performance such as electric safety, lightweight design and range. The presence of batteries and electric motors poses different risks in collision accidents. Side impact is a critical crash event where the vehicle is crashed by a movable car or vehicle may hit a tree or pole. Side impact collision of vehicle is one of the awfully hazardous crashes causing injuries and death annually around the world. In side pole crash, vehicle rocker or sill structure plays an important role in resisting the load due to the crash.
      Recent research suggests that in order to reduce the risk of battery explosion, an impact energy absorbing structure, i.e. a crash element inside the sill beam can be developed. Therefore, this thesis work focuses on the development of an optimised side sill structure along with the reinforcements for the protection of battery system of an electric vehicle. The main objective of the thesis is to present a systematic and practical methodology to design an crash element inside a side sill beam in order to reinforce the crushing behaviour during a lateral vehicle impact. The mechanical responses are simulated in commercial software such as CATIA, ANSYS 18.2 and LS-DYNA.
      It is however not practical to undertake such comprehensive investigation each time a sill structure of all geometries is to be made available. Hence, numerical models, i.e. finite element models that can represent the relationship between crash-worthiness parameters and deformation in simple mathematical terms is desirable. Therefore, static and dynamic development and construction of several side sill structure with crash element members and evaluation of the potentials with regard to energy absorption characteristics are taken into account. This research work also investigates the effect of variations in the thickness, impact angle, mode of collapse, apart from the geometric variations, in a three-point bending subjected to both static and dynamic loading using finite element method.
      The major crash-worthiness indicators, such as Energy absorption, Specific energy absorption and Crash force efficiency are the key parameters on which this comparison is made. It is concluded that different configuration set-ups can be selected for achieving better crashworthiness criteria in typical side crash scenario like a EURO NCAP side pole impact. It can also be concluded that there is potential for mass reduction. The results show that the crashworthiness performance of side sill structure can be greatly improved through introduction of proper adjustments in the geometry, thickness, foams, mode of collapse and proper direction of impact. Using the proposed methodologies, the current research presents a fundamental and systematic study to conduct design a side sill structure along with the crash elements of EV’s practically and efficiently. Further investigation into the assembly feasibility is suggested as future work.

      Tasks involved
    • Literature research of the current protection concepts for battery systems in the event of a crash.
    • Analysis of the existing integration concept of a crash structure within the side sill and identification of necessary change requirements.
    • Selection of a suitable crash structure for effective absorption of crash energy and power line on the defined load paths within the battery pack.
    • Construction of the selected concept in CAD and modelling of a lateral vehicle impact by means of a suitably reduced FE model.
    • Implementation of fitting and correction strategies through comprehensive error analysis.
    • Identification of optimization potentials of the designed battery pack structure.

    Master's project: EV Battery pack safety

    Crashworthiness analysis of the modular battery pack considering various geometries and its influence on energy absorption characteristics.

      About the project
      The automotive industry is changing. Especially since the diesel scandal and its consequences, e- mobility has gained attention and importance not only in society but also among manufacturers themselves. As a clean energy technology, the development of electric vehicles (EVs) is challenged by lightweight design, battery safety and range. Fundamentally, cells within the pack need to be protected from adverse situations that could be electrical, mechanical, or thermal in nature. In this study, the simulations indicate that using a flexible structure of battery module has the potential to overcome the limitations in battery-powered EVs, contributing to a new design.
      Specifically, we focus on optimizing the structure of vehicle battery packs, aiming to improve the crash-worthiness of EVs through side impact crash simulations. Further, the key safety features of existing EV battery packs have been closely studied using some sources including scientific and technical literature and particularly patents. However, a deep understanding of the relationship of volumetric efficiency with space occupancy rate is essential for developing a battery pack structure. Hence, volumetric efficiency and space occupancy rate of each module and packs are estimated in this work. To improve the crash-worthiness and maximise passenger protection, the optimum design is selected with respect to the structure of battery packs.
      The mechanical responses are simulated in commercial software such as ANSYS 18.2 and LS-DYNA. It is however not practical to undertake such comprehensive investigation each time a battery pack of all geometries is to be made available. Hence, numerical models that can represent the relationship between crash-worthiness parameters and deformation in simple mathematical terms is desirable. Therefore, static and dynamic development and construction of several crash-deformable battery module/packs designs and evaluation of the potentials with regard to energy absorption characteristics are taken into account.
      In addition, assessment of the potential of the developed design based on the load conditions of a lateral pole impact. A type of hexagonal battery pack is proposed to improve energy absorption performance, which is inspired by the investigation showing the significant advantage of hexagonal over other geometries in energy absorption characteristics. In addition, a parametric study considering the effects of mass variation on the structural crash-worthiness has been carried out. And it is found that increased mass has a distinct and least effect on the energy absorption characteristics, whereas the geometric variation produces a better and optimised results as compared to the commercially available one. Finally, the basic mechanical design requirements for a robust and reliable battery pack structure are revealed.

      Tasks involved
    • Definition of the theoretical basics of the battery electric drive train as well as the current active and passive safety devices.
    • Development and construction of several crash-deformable battery module designs and evaluation of the potentials with regard to energy absorption and energy density.
    • Assessment of the potential of the developed design based on the load conditions of a lateral pole impact.
    • Characterizing the relation between the deformation and volume aspects of the selected battery packs.
    • Derivation of possible design guidelines for the design of crash deformable battery modules.

      Tools used : ANSYS 18 , LS DYNA , GEOGEBRA , LaTeX

    Bachelor Thesis: Heat Exchanger

    Comprehensive study on segmental and helical baffled shell and tube heat exchanger

      About the project
      In the thesis, experiments on segmental & amp; helical baffled shell and tube heat exchanger have done and with that data, theoretical calculations were done which was in accordance with the journals. The methods for these theoretical calculations were Kern, Bell and Bell –Delaware methods. Later a comparison was done between segmental and helical baffled shell and tube heat exchangers with respect to the heat transfer coefficient by Kern method alone. Attempts were also made to investigate the impacts of various baffle inclination angles on fluid flow and the heat transfer characteristics of a helical baffled shell-and-tube heat exchanger for two different baffle inclination angles namely 20° and 40°. The study is concerned with a single shell and single side pass parallel flow heat exchanger.
      The flow and temperature fields inside the shell are studied using non-commercial computational fluid dynamics software tool ANSYS workbench 14.0 for FLUID FLOW (FLUENT). For a given baffle, the heat exchanger performance is investigated by varying mass flow rate and baffle inclination angle. From the computational fluid dynamics simulation results, the shell side outlet temperature, pressure drop, re-circulation near the baffles, optimal mass flow rate and the optimum baffle inclination angle for the given heat exchanger geometry are determined.

      Tools used : SOLIDWORKS, ANSYS (Fluent), MATLAB

Code

  • "Automated crack advancement and detection using SEAM tool in ABAQUS by python scripts"
      • This python code helps to detect crack as well as create crack advancement using SEAM tool in ABAQUS and generates corresponding meshes in each time steps.

  • "StressCrackGen - Cracks generated using Stress Criterion"
      • StressCrackGen is a Python project developed to automate stress analysis and crack detection in finite element models. The project utilizes various algorithms to identify potential crack regions based on stress criteria and generate crack paths accordingly. The main functionality of StressCrackGen revolves around the Main_Script_SC_0.py script, which serves as the entry point for executing the stress analysis and crack detection pipeline. When running the Main_Script_SC_0.py script, the following tasks are performed:
    • StressCrackGen extracts stress data from an ABAQUS finite element model and combines it with corresponding node coordinates. The global maximum principal stress and local sigma theta values are utilized to define the stress criterion. However, the project provides flexibility for users to select alternative stress-based criteria to generate crack paths.
    • The crack detection process involves identifying crack regions using the DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise) algorithm. DBSCAN allows for efficient extraction of clusters in the stress data, helping to identify different crack regions. Additionally, the K-means clustering algorithm is employed to generate crack paths based on the extracted crack regions.
    • StressCrackGen generates output files that include stress data, crack regions, and crack paths. These outputs can be further visualized using 2D or 3D plotting techniques, providing a comprehensive view of the stress analysis and crack detection results. The StressCrackGen project aims to streamline the process of stress analysis and crack detection, providing researchers and engineers with a powerful tool for evaluating structural integrity and identifying potential failure modes. By automating these tasks, StressCrackGen enables efficient and accurate analysis, saving time and effort in manual inspection.
      Algorithms Used - The StressCrackGen project employs the following algorithms:
    • DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise): DBSCAN is utilized to identify different crack regions based on the critical sigma property of the material. This algorithm allows for efficient extraction of clusters in the stress data, aiding in the identification of crack regions.
    • K-means: The K-means clustering algorithm is employed to generate crack paths based on the extracted crack regions. By grouping crack regions into clusters, StressCrackGen can determine the crack paths.

  • "ECO (Energy Cracks Optimiser) Framework"
      • The ECO Framework is designed for optimizing energy cracks in a simulation environment. ECO Python module optimizes cracks generated by the stress criterion module using the Alternating Minimization Algorithm (AMA). AMA fine-tunes crack parameters iteratively, ensuring efficiency in the optimization process, and critical cracks are determined based on the energy criterion. ECO selectively activates in regions processed by the stress criterion, efficiently saving computational time and costs. It provides a set of modules, utilities, and tools for pre-processing, simulations, and result analysis.
